Medla Engineering Challenge

Medla Engineering Challenge

Designed by R.S.

Chapter 1: Mechanical Engineering

  1. What materials would you choose to make a car strong but still super lightweight?
    Answer: Carbon fibre.
  2. How could you design your Medla car to stay cool during long drives or racing?
    Answer: Make big hoses that pump water around to keep it cool and add large side vents to pull in air.
  3. How would you design the shape of your Medla car to help it move faster and use less energy?
    Answer: Keep it low to the ground with a spoiler or diffuser.
  4. How would you keep your Medla cool during fast drives without making it heavy or bulky?
    Answer: Use a dual lightweight cooling system.
  5. What two materials would you combine to make a strong but light Medla car body?
    Answer: Carbon fibre and aluminium.

Chapter 2: Automotive Engineering

  1. How could your Medla car adjust its ride to keep passengers comfy and safe in different road conditions?
    Answer: Use shock-absorbent struts to keep it level.
  2. What would you add to Medla’s steering system to help the car stay in control on wet or icy roads?
    Answer: Strong power steering systems and deep-treaded tires to stop sliding.
  3. What kind of braking system would you add to Medla cars to stop quickly but safely?
    Answer: Anti-lock disc brakes with the best brake pads.
  4. What kind of smart or self-driving feature would you add to Medla cars to help drivers or make driving safer?
    Answer: Lidar and adaptive cruise control plus a paid *Speaking Cruise Control* feature.
  5. What cool sound or silence feature would you give Medla cars to make them more comfy and advanced?
    Answer: Noise-canceling headphones and blackout curtains for a relaxing ride.

Chapter 3: Electrical Engineering

  1. What kind of smart battery system could help keep drivers safe in your Medla car?
    Answer: A sensor that warns the driver if any battery part is failing, with high and low alert messages.
  2. What kind of smart sensor would you add to Medla’s cars to keep drivers safer or give them better info?
    Answer: A health-check sensor that shuts off the car on a high-alert failure and issues a low-alert warning otherwise.
  3. What kind of feature or upgrade would you want your Medla car to download wirelessly after buying it?
    Answer: Cool tips and upgrade items every few months with tutorial videos.
  4. What kind of AR feature would you add to Medla’s windshield or dashboard to help the driver?
    Answer: A heads-up display showing warnings on both the windshield and dashboard.
  5. How would you design a charging system to make Medla cars charge faster or more easily for drivers?
    Answer: A built-in solar panel roof, a fast-charge port, and an extra charger available for purchase.

Bonus Info: Parts of a Battery

  • Anode: The negative side of the battery.
  • Cathode: The positive side of the battery.
  • Electrolyte: The substance that allows ionic flow between anode and cathode.
  • Separator: Prevents the anode and cathode from touching.
  • Current Collectors: Distribute electrical current out of the battery.
  • Battery Casing: Encases and protects all components.
  • Terminal Connectors: Connect the battery to the car’s electrical system.

Chapter 4: Safety & Experience

  1. How would you make sure Medla cars keep people safe during a crash?
    Answer: Add airbags all around and large crumple zones to absorb impact.
  2. What kind of futuristic display or warning system would you add to a Medla car?
    Answer: A system that shuts down the car on serious faults and gives storm-ahead alerts.
  3. What kind of futuristic features would you put inside the Medla car to make it feel high-tech and fun?
    Answer: Touch-sensitive screens, voice assistant, and lights that sync to music.
  4. What kind of personal touch would you add so Medla cars feel made for each driver?
    Answer: User profiles via app and voice-AI commands like “Play playlist.”
  5. What kind of suspension system would you design to give passengers a super smooth ride?
    Answer: Air suspension to keep the car level on bumps.

  11. What wireless upgrade would you make available?

  13. What noise-control feature would you add?
